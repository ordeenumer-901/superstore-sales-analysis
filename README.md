# 📊 Superstore Sales Analysis

**Tools:** Python · pandas · SQLite · SQL · Chart.js  
**Data:** 8,547 orders · 2014–2017 · 4 regions · 3 product categories  
**Goal:** Answer 5 business questions that drive real decisions

---

## 🧠 Business Problem

> *"We have 4 years of sales data. Where are we making money, where are we losing it, and what should we do differently next quarter?"*

This project mirrors the kind of analysis a junior data analyst would be asked to produce on their first week. Instead of just cleaning data and making charts, every step is framed around a business decision.

## ❓ The 5 Business Questions

| # | Question | Key Finding |
|---|----------|-------------|
| Q1 | Is revenue growing year-over-year? | 2016 was peak revenue ($604K). 2017 shows highest margin ever (52.2%). |
| Q2 | Which product categories and sub-categories drive the most profit? | Cutting Edge leads margin (51.5%). Xbox Games is #1 sub-category by profit. |
| Q3 | Which regions are over/underperforming? | Central has the best margin (54.7%) but fewest customers — a growth opportunity. |
| Q4 | Who are our most valuable customers? | Top 3 customers average 80%+ margin vs. company average of 50.5%. |
| Q5 | Are discounts hurting our margins? | Margins hold well across tiers. But 788 orders at 50%+ discount need review. |

---

## 💡 Key Insights & Recommendations

### 1. Push growth in the Central Region
Central has the highest profit margin (54.7%) but the fewest orders (1,994 vs West's 2,732). Sales effort invested here would yield the highest-quality returns.

### 2. Protect your top 3 customers
Sean Miller, Tamara Chand, and Sanjit Chand each deliver 80%+ margins with consistent order frequency. These accounts warrant dedicated relationship management.

### 3. Review all discounts above 50%
788 orders — nearly 9% of all orders — were discounted at 50% or more. The data also flags a 122% maximum discount, which likely represents a data entry error or a serious pricing decision that needs documentation.

### 4. 2017 efficiency story
Revenue dipped from 2016 to 2017, but margin improved to 52.2%. This isn't necessarily bad — it could signal a shift toward higher-quality orders. Worth comparing product mix between years.
